# About the WMRN Radio History Archive

The WMRN Radio History Archive is an independent historical preservation initiative dedicated to documenting, preserving, and organizing the broadcast legacy of WMRN-AM (1490) and its associated FM operations in Marion, Ohio.

Since signing on in December 1940, WMRN has served north-central Ohio through news, music, sports coverage, emergency broadcasting, public service programming, and community engagement. The station’s impact extends beyond its frequency. It lives in the voices, stories, and shared memories of the community it served.

This archive exists to ensure that history is not lost.

---

## Mission

To preserve, document, and make accessible materials related to the broadcast history of WMRN for present and future generations.

---

## Scope of the Archive

The Archive collects and maintains materials relating to:

- The founding and operational history of WMRN  
- On-air personalities, management, engineers, and staff  
- Programming, promotions, and public service initiatives  
- Studio facilities, equipment, and technical evolution  
- Audio recordings, photographs, printed materials, and memorabilia  
- Community events and emergency coverage  

Primary historical coverage begins in 1940 and continues through the present era.

---

## Preservation Philosophy

Broadcast history is inherently fragile. Magnetic tape deteriorates. Photographs fade. Documents are discarded. Institutional memory disappears as generations move on.

The WMRN Radio History Archive is committed to:

- Structured accessioning of materials  
- Accurate metadata documentation  
- Responsible stewardship of digital and physical artifacts  
- Clear documentation of rights and restrictions  
- Long-term preservation practices  

Preservation, not reinterpretation, is the guiding principle.

---

## Independence

The WMRN Radio History Archive is privately maintained and operates independently.

It is not affiliated with, endorsed by, or operated by the current or former owners of WMRN, WMRN-FM, or any parent company. Materials are presented for historical and educational purposes.

Independence allows the archive to focus solely on preservation and documentation.

---

## Research & Use

Materials are made available for research, reference, and educational use.

Some items may carry access or reproduction restrictions based on copyright, donor agreements, or preservation considerations. Researchers and media outlets are encouraged to review the **[Access & Use Policy](/access-and-use/)** for citation standards and permissions guidance.

---

## Contributions & Historical Accuracy

The Archive is built through a combination of public records, private collections, and community contributions.

While every effort is made to verify dates, names, and events, historical records are occasionally incomplete or contradictory. Corrections, clarifications, and additional documentation are welcomed in the interest of accuracy.

If you possess photographs, recordings, documents, or contextual information relevant to WMRN’s history, contributions are encouraged.

---

## Stewardship

The Archive is curated by a former WMRN staff member with both professional experience in broadcasting and a long-standing commitment to preserving the station’s legacy.

The project is guided by respect for:

- The individuals who shaped the station  
- The community it served  
- The historical record itself  

The intention is permanence.
