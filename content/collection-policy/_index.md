## Purpose

The WMRN Radio History Archive exists to preserve, document, and make accessible materials related to the broadcast history of WMRN-AM (1490) and its associated stations in Marion, Ohio.

This Collection Development Policy defines the scope, standards, and criteria by which materials are evaluated, acquired, and preserved.

---

## Scope of the Collection

The Archive collects materials that directly relate to:

- WMRN-AM (1490), Marion, Ohio
- WMRN-FM and subsequent FM frequency changes, including 94.3 MHz
- Station staff, management, and on-air personalities
- Studio facilities and equipment
- Programming, promotions, and public service initiatives
- Community events, emergency coverage, and notable broadcasts
- Advertising materials and station-branded ephemera
- Photographs, audio recordings, documents, and physical artifacts

Primary coverage begins with WMRN’s founding in December 1940 and continues through the present day.

---

## Types of Materials Collected

The Archive may accept, in physical or digital form:

- Audio recordings (broadcast segments, promos, interviews, airchecks)
- Photographs (studios, staff, equipment, events)
- Printed materials (ads, scripts, internal documents)
- Station-branded materials and memorabilia
- Equipment and studio artifacts of historical relevance
- Oral history recordings
- Correspondence related to station operations

---

## Materials Not Typically Collected

The Archive generally does not collect:

- Materials unrelated to WMRN or its direct affiliates
- Items lacking sufficient historical context or provenance
- Duplicates of materials already preserved, unless superior in quality
- Items with unresolved ownership or copyright concerns

---

## Acquisition & Accessioning

All materials accepted into the Archive are assigned a unique Accession ID following the structured format:

`WMRN-YYYY-###`

Each accession record includes:

- Title
- Date or estimated year
- Media type
- Summary description
- Source or donor information
- Rights status
- Physical and/or digital location
- Condition assessment
- Access restrictions, if applicable

Accession records are maintained as part of the permanent archival record.

---

## Rights & Permissions

Ownership of physical materials does not automatically transfer copyright.

The Archive documents rights status at the time of accession and respects:

- Donor agreements
- Copyright law
- Usage restrictions specified by contributors

Materials may be marked as Restricted where appropriate.

---

## Preservation Commitment

Digital materials are stored in structured formats with redundancy and metadata preservation.

Physical materials, when retained, are stored in controlled environments whenever possible.

The Archive is committed to long-term preservation and responsible stewardship.

---

## Amendments

This Collection Development Policy may be revised as the Archive grows and its preservation standards evolve.
