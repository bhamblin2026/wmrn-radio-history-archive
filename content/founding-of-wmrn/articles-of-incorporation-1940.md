# Articles of Incorporation  
## The Marion Broadcasting Company  
Filed April 22, 1940

On April 22, 1940, The Marion Broadcasting Company was formally incorporated under the laws of the State of Ohio. This filing established the legal corporate entity that would construct and operate radio station WMRN in Marion, Ohio.

At the time of incorporation, radio broadcasting was entering one of its most influential eras. Local stations served as primary sources of news, weather, civic information, and national programming. The formation of The Marion Broadcasting Company placed Marion within this expanding communications network during a period of rapid technological and social change.

The incorporation was recorded in Volume 471, Page 131, in the official records of the Ohio Secretary of State.

The company was authorized to issue 250 shares of no par value stock and began operations with a declared capitalization of $15,000 — a significant investment for a regional broadcasting enterprise in 1940.

The incorporators were:

- Robert T. Mason  
- Fred E. Guthery  
- Howard F. Guthery  

---

## Corporate Structure and Authority

The Articles granted the corporation broad operational authority, including:

- Owning and operating radio broadcasting stations  
- Operating television and facsimile services  
- Conducting advertising and promotional business  
- Buying, leasing, and managing real estate  
- Entering into contracts necessary to carry out corporate purposes  

Notably, the document references television broadcasting in 1940 — several years before television became commercially widespread — reflecting the forward-looking legal language common in corporate filings of the period.

The authorization of 250 shares of no par value stock provided structural flexibility in ownership distribution, while the $15,000 capitalization represented the financial foundation for equipment acquisition, studio construction, licensing, and transmission infrastructure.

This filing represents the legal birth of what would become WMRN.

### Articles of Incorporation  
**The Marion Broadcasting Company**

The undersigned, a majority of whom are citizens of the United States, desiring to form a corporation, for profit, under the General Corporation Act of Ohio, do hereby certify:

**FIRST.** The name of said corporation shall be The Marion Broadcasting Company.

**SECOND.** The place in Ohio where its principal office is to be located is Marion, Marion County.

**THIRD.** The purpose or purposes for which it is formed are:

Owning, leasing and operating radio broadcasting, television and facsimile stations; disseminating advertising, entertainment and amusements of all kinds and in any manner; carrying on a general advertising business; buying, selling, leasing and operating real estate and buildings therein; executing, making, giving and performing any and every agreement or contract necessary or desirable for carrying on any or all of the business of said corporation; and doing everything necessary, suitable or proper for the accomplishment of the purposes herein enumerated and which shall at any time appear expedient for the benefit or protection of said corporation, or its rights, property or assets, or calculated to enhance the value thereof; and to do any and all of the things herein set forth to the same extent as a natural person might or could do, and in furtherance and not in limitation of the general powers conferred by the laws of the State of Ohio; and also to do and perform all other things that it legally may do.

**FOURTH.** The maximum number of shares which the corporation is authorized to have outstanding is two hundred fifty (250), all of which shall be with no par value.

**FIFTH.** The amount of capital with which the corporation will begin business is Fifteen Thousand Dollars ($15,000.00).

IN WITNESS WHEREOF, We have hereunto subscribed our names this 22nd day of April, 1940.

Robert T. Mason  
Fred E. Guthery  
Howard F. Guthery  

---

### Notarial Acknowledgment

State of Ohio, County of Marion, ss:

Enid Little
