<div class="exhibit-content">

On April 22, 1940, The Marion Broadcasting Company was formally incorporated under the laws of the State of Ohio. This filing established the legal corporate entity that would construct and operate radio station WMRN in Marion, Ohio.

At the time of incorporation, radio broadcasting was entering one of its most influential eras. Local stations served as primary sources of news, weather, civic information, and national programming. The formation of The Marion Broadcasting Company placed Marion within this expanding communications network during a period of rapid technological and social change.

The company was authorized to issue 250 shares of no par value stock and began operations with a declared capitalization of $15,000 — a significant investment for a regional broadcasting enterprise in 1940.

This filing represents the legal birth of what would become WMRN.

## Corporate Structure and Authority

The Articles granted the corporation broad operational authority, including:

- Owning and operating radio broadcasting stations  
- Operating television and facsimile services  
- Conducting advertising and promotional business  
- Buying, leasing, and managing real estate  
- Entering into contracts necessary to carry out corporate purposes  

Notably, the document references television broadcasting in 1940 — several years before television became commercially widespread — reflecting the forward-looking legal language common in corporate filings of the period.
