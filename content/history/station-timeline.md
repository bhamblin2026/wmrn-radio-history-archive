# Station Timeline

This timeline presents a structured chronological record of major documented milestones in the history of **WMRN-AM (1490)** and **WMRN-FM** in Marion, Ohio.

Dates reflect verified regulatory filings, documented broadcasts, and confirmed historical sources.

---

## 1940s

**December 1940**  
WMRN-AM (1490) signs on the air in Marion, Ohio, establishing a locally licensed AM broadcast service.

---

## 1950s

**May 6, 1953 — 6:30 a.m.**  
WMRN-FM signs on the air at **106.9 MHz**, expanding the station’s broadcast footprint into FM transmission and marking one of the earlier FM operations in the region.

---

## 1960s

**1969**  
The WMRN Peanut Push begins, establishing a recurring community initiative associated with the station.

---

## 1970s

**1978**  
WMRN provides extended emergency coverage during the Blizzard of 1978, reinforcing the station’s role in severe weather broadcasting.

**1979**  
First documented on-air appearance of Buckeye Chuck as part of Groundhog Day programming.

---

## 1980s

**April 1, 1981**  
WMRN-FM transitions to a **Country music format**, marking a significant programming shift and establishing a format identity that would define the FM station’s audience focus during the decade.

Expansion of local news coverage, sports broadcasting, and promotional campaigns continue across both AM and FM operations.

---

## 1990s

Operational transitions, production expansion, and continued local programming define the decade.

---

## 2000s

**January 6, 2008**  
WMRN-FM transitions to **94.3 MHz**, marking a significant technical and branding milestone in the station’s FM operations.

---

## 2010s

Continued modernization of facilities, format adjustments, and digital adaptation in response to industry changes.

---

## 2020s

Establishment of the WMRN Radio History Archive as an independent preservation initiative documenting the station’s historical record.

---

This timeline serves as a structured chronological reference and will expand as additional ownership transitions, licensing changes, programming launches, and technical developments are verified and accessioned.
